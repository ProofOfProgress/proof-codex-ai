Prompt 3 | Misleading / Unsupported Claim

When they claim your video made false or exaggerated claims.

Add a screenshot of your violation to ChatGPT along with the prompt below. It will write an appeal under 500 characters (as TikTok requires) and tell you exactly which screenshots to attach. Or just use the Violation Appeal Agent™ to do it all automatically.
Copy
Please provide me with a violation appeal for the attached screenshot that is under 500 characters, respectful but stern. The violation appears to claim that my video made misleading, exaggerated, unsupported, or inaccurate product claims. The appeal should firmly state that the video accurately represented the product based on the product listing, visible product details, and truthful presentation, without making false or deceptive claims.

Do not state or imply that I may have committed the violation. The appeal should close by requesting a manual review of the violation and complete reinstatement of the product link on the violated video once the violation is withdrawn.

After the appeal, provide a separate list of screenshots I should attach to support the appeal. Before listing the screenshots, include this exact statement:
“Please note: The screenshots listed below are optional, but recommended. Not all of the screenshots listed below need to be linked, but all are viable and will be beneficial to getting your violation withdrawn.”

The screenshot list should include items such as:
1. Screenshot of the violation notice
2. Screenshot of the exact claim or statement made in the video
3. Screenshot of the product listing supporting that claim
4. Screenshot of product features, specifications, or ingredients
5. Screenshot of seller-provided product details
6. Screenshot of the product in my TikTok Shop showcase
7. Screenshot of the product packaging or label if it supports the claim
8. Screenshot showing that the video wording stayed consistent with the listing

End the attachment section with a short sentence explaining which images should not be attached. For this violation, do not recommend attaching screenshots of claims that are not supported by the product listing, exaggerated before-and-after results, unrelated customer reviews, or personal ID verification screenshots unless TikTok specifically requests identity verification.
