### 🥇 Recommendation: **AI Tool Verdicts**

| Level | Name |
|-------|------|
| Big | AI / Tech |
| Sub | **AI tools for normal people** (not developers) |
| Sub-sub | **"I tested it so you don't have to"** — 30-second honest verdicts |

**One-line pitch:** Every day, one hyped AI product gets a fast, skeptical verdict from a real person — pay, skip, or wait.

**Why I like this (my reasons, not yours):**
1. **News never runs out** — new AI tools ship weekly; you're not inventing topics, you're filtering noise.
2. **The gap on YouTube is honesty** — 90% of AI Shorts are hype ("THIS CHANGES EVERYTHING"). Skeptical "here's the catch" is underserved and shareable.
3. **Your twin is the product** — trust face + voice beats faceless listicles. InVideo was built for talking head + B-roll.
4. **RPM** — tech/tools Shorts sit in a better band than gaming/horror (~$0.05–0.20 US directional).
5. **Course ladder later** — "Don't get scammed by AI tools" / "How to evaluate AI before you pay" is a natural paid product once you have 50+ verdicts.
6. **Automation-native** — script template is fixed; agent pulls tool name + 3 claims + your verdict; no beat-sync, no custom visuals.

**Format (repeat every Short):**
- **0–3s hook:** "Everyone's paying for [X]. I tested it."
- **3–20s:** One real limitation (privacy, price, quality, lock-in) — not a feature list.
- **20–28s:** Verdict: **Pay / Skip / Wait for v2** + one sentence why.
- **28–30s:** CTA: "Follow so you don't waste money on the next one."

**Example titles (first 2 weeks):**
- "I paid for ChatGPT Pro so you don't have to — skip it if…"
- "That AI headshot app? Here's what they don't show you."
- "InVideo vs CapCut AI — honest 30-second pick"
- "This 'AI girlfriend' app is worse than you think"
- "NotebookLM is free and actually useful — here's the one catch"

**What we are NOT:** prompt spam ("5 ChatGPT hacks"), developer tutorials, or fake "I made $10k with AI."

### 🥈 Strong alternative: **AI Myths Busted**

| Level | Name |
|-------|------|
| Big | AI / Tech |
| Sub | **AI literacy** (how it actually works) |
| Sub-sub | **One scary or surprising truth per Short** — what companies don't say on the landing page |

**One-line pitch:** One misconception about AI, destroyed in 30 seconds — the stuff your non-tech friends get wrong.

**Why it's good:**
- Evergreen (less dependent on weekly product launches)
- You already know this space better than average viewers
- High save/share ("send this to your mom")
- Less competitive than "AI side hustle" garbage

**Example titles:**
- "AI can't count — here's proof in 10 seconds"
- "Your ChatGPT chats aren't private — read the fine print"
- "Voice clones need 3 seconds, not 30 minutes"
- "That 'AI detected' essay checker is mostly guessing"
- "Free AI tools sell your data — here's how to tell"

**Risk:** Can feel lecture-y without strong hooks. Needs your personality on camera.

3. **Declare synthetic presenter** when using AI twin (YPP-safe pattern)
4. **No template spam** — vary hooks; same *shape*, different *words*
5. **1 Short / 24h** until analytics say otherwise
