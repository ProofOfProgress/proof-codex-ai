# Peripheral — world bible

Every Short is **one episode** in the same world — **The Gap**, where recordings lag behind reality and catch what your eyes miss.

## Horror lane (locked)

**Primary: Analog CCTV horror** — fullscreen security footage; night-vision rooms; replays that show impossible movement.

**Secondary: Apartment glitch** — mirrors, closets, hallways, alarm clocks, and alone-at-night domestic spaces.

**Tertiary: Peripheral motion** — something moves at the edge of frame; the final replay becomes a jumpscare.

## The world in one sentence

**Peripheral** is an apartment where the recording lags behind reality — the camera catches the thing beside you before you feel it breathe.

## The Gap (the rule)

- **Core rule:** recordings lag behind reality and reveal what the character missed
- **Glitch hour:** 3:12 AM
- **Proof objects:** CCTV playback, mirror reflection, alarm clock, closet gap, hallway corner
- **Threat:** edge-of-frame motion that becomes undeniable in the final beat
- **Visibility:** tease in shadows first; full reveal only on the final jumpscare

## The apartment

- Alone-at-night apartment, CCTV angle, door crack, closet, mirror, hallway, alarm clock
- Fullscreen security-cam drafts only — no fake phone UI taking over the frame
- Time via alarm clock or REC overlay
- Night-vision green, cold blue-black, dirty monitor whites; crimson only for finale

## Episode laws

| Law | Scripts & visuals |
|-----|-------------------|
| **Immediate POV** | You/first-person intimacy; no cosy explanation |
| **Proof object** | CCTV/replay/mirror/alarm clock shows the impossible detail |
| **Lag rule** | The recording disagrees with what the character remembers |
| **One broken rule** | Hook = premise (camera flagged motion; mirror blinked; closet gap changed) |
| **Twist → sting** | Final beat rewrites hook — then stop |
| **Don't blink** | Merch tagline — movement in peripheral vision, thing at frame edge |

## Story voice

- Concrete, immediate, visual — the viewer knows exactly what the camera caught
- False calm = rationalization failing — "maybe it was a compression glitch" — then wrong detail returns
- Twist → sting → **stop** — no explanation after

## Merch

- **Solid white hoodie** — huge line-eye on back, *don't blink* underneath
- Brand name **PERIPHERAL** only — no abbreviations on garments

## What is NOT this world

- Village cult lore, folk rituals, dream Eye mythology
- Faceless narrator listicle Shorts
- Cosy self-help, stick figures, generic creepypasta listicles
- Bright daytime crowds, camping slashers, real-world religion mockery

## Why viewers binge

1. **What did the camera catch?** — proof beats reward rewatching
2. **Will it move again?** — peripheral motion trains viewers not to blink
3. **Same apartment grammar** — mirror, closet, hallway, alarm clock, replay
4. **Final jumpscare** — completion incentive every Short
