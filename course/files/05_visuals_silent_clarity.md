# 05 — Visuals, Filming, and Silent Clarity

Use when the creator needs to know how the video should look and what visual choices should carry the story.

## Semantic anchors
visuals, props, mute, silent viewing, filming, phone camera, production, first frame, clarity

## Visuals over sound
- People see before they hear; visuals hook attention first.
- Visuals tell the story faster than words.
- Visuals create emotion quickly and cross language barriers.
- The video should still work when watched on mute.

## Visual checklist
- Make the first frame visually compelling.
- Tell the story through action, expressions, bold imagery, or visual metaphors.
- Use text overlays only to support clarity, not to carry the whole idea.
- Review the video on mute before uploading.

## Phone-first filming
- Shorts do not need to look cinematic to perform well.
- Use 4K and highest quality settings available.
- Lighting matters; use rule of thirds; keep text/visuals in safe zone.

## Relatability filter
Do not chase cinematic polish if it makes the content feel less relatable.
