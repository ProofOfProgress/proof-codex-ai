# 03 — Blue Ocean and Channel Positioning

Use when the creator is asking how to stand out, find a lane, or avoid feeling generic in a crowded niche.

## Semantic anchors
stand out, saturation, channel angle, niche, blue ocean, positioning, differentiation, ownable lane

## Red ocean vs blue ocean
- **Red ocean:** Crowded space; creators fight for the same attention.
- **Blue ocean:** Fresher, less crowded concept or format; demand is created instead of fought over.

## Differentiation levers
- Find what is missing in the niche.
- Add a unique twist to a familiar idea.
- Reinvent the format instead of only changing the topic.
- Target an underserved audience.
- Blend niches together to create a category of one.

## Practical examples
- Cooking: history of food instead of standard tutorials.
- Fitness: workouts + video game themes.
- Real estate: game-like house tour instead of normal tour.
- Fashion: serve an overlooked audience.

## Positioning check
- What gives someone a reason to watch you instead of an established creator?
- What angle, execution style, or audience choice makes the work ownable?
