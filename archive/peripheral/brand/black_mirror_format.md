# Peripheral — Black Mirror episode feel

Every Short should land like **one episode of Black Mirror compressed to ~30 seconds** — not a random jumpscare clip.

## What that means (plain English)

| Black Mirror | Peripheral Short |
|--------------|------------------|
| One sharp **"what if"** premise | Hook line 1 states the broken rule |
| Ordinary person, cold camera | Second-person **you**, cinematic dread |
| Consequences **escalate** | Beats 2–6 — each beat costs more |
| **Twist** rewrites what you thought was happening | Beat 7 — new truth, not a lecture |
| Lingering unease after credits | Final **sting** (lunge/glitch) — then **stop**, no explanation |
| Anthology — episodes share **grammar**, not one plot | Rotate settings (apartment, village, warehouse pit) |

## 30-second episode map

```
0–3s   PREMISE     — one impossible rule ("if you see the sign, you die — slowly")
3–12s  NORMAL      — you rationalize; world looks almost fine
12–20s ESCALATION  — the rule bites; symptoms, timestamps, witnesses fail
20–26s FALSE CALM  — quiet dread; you almost believe it's nothing
26–30s TWIST+STING — recontextualize hook → jumpscare lands on the **new** truth
```

## Twist types (rotate)

1. **It was always you** — victim was the observer all along
2. **The rule was already broken** — you triggered it before the video started
3. **Recording was the trap** — CCTV/mirror/feed is the weapon, not proof
4. **Mercy was the horror** — the "kind" choice was the worst one
5. **The village remembers** — collective rule everyone pretends not to know

## Voice rules

- Clinical, precise nouns — not purple creepypasta prose
- No "scary story #12", no cosy therapy tone
- **Never explain the twist after the sting** — let comments argue
- Volume-warning finale still applies — sting is earned by the twist, not random noise

## Settings (anthology — same episode grammar)

- **The Gap** — liminal apartment, lag, mirrors, CCTV (existing bible)
- **The village** — see the sign/monster → guaranteed death; slow sickness after (YPP-safe: implied, not graphic)
- **The pit** — industrial metal theatre, masks, warehouse ritual (symbolic only)
