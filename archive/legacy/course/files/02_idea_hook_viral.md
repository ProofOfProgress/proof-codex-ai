# 02 — Idea, Hook, and Viral Evaluation

Use when the creator needs to judge whether an idea is actually strong enough to carry a video.

## Semantic anchors
idea strength, viral idea, good idea, weak concept, hookable, evaluate idea, best idea, compare ideas

## Idea first principles
- The idea is the foundation; a unique idea cuts through noise.
- A strong idea drives attention, rewatchability, and shares.
- Execution matters, but a weak idea will fail regardless.
- Most effort should be spent brainstorming and refining ideas before execution.

## Hook and idea are linked
Jenny treats the idea and hook as strategically linked. If it can't be hooked, the idea needs reshaping. Brainstorm both at the same time.

## Viral idea checklist
- Does it grab attention instantly?
- Does it spark emotion?
- Is it shareable?
- Is it unexpected or unique?
- Does it encourage rewatching?
- Does it have broad appeal or connect to a universal theme?
- Is it easy to explain?
- Is there a strong payoff?

## How to score a concept
**Weak:** Bland before execution, confusing to explain, needs cinematic polish to be interesting.
**Strong:** Clear emotional pull, visual potential, payoff tension.

## Hook template snapshot
- Start with shock or immediate curiosity.
- Set expectation quickly.
- Give a reason to watch until the end.
- Start the video as soon as possible.
