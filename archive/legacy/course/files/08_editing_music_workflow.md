# 08 — Editing, Music, and Workflow

Use when the creator needs to edit faster, simplify the process, or choose music that supports the story without emotional whiplash.

## Semantic anchors
editing, music, workflow, software, shortcuts, over-editing, pacing, soundtrack, toolstack

## Editing principles
- Plan ahead before editing.
- Know the story structure and key beats.
- Make the last take the best take so editing is faster.
- Learn keyboard shortcuts.
- Keep editing simple — simpler shorts often feel more relatable.

## Software notes from the transcript
- Adobe Premiere Pro: advanced, industry standard.
- Final Cut Pro: strong for Mac users.
- DaVinci Resolve: feature-rich, strong for visuals (free version available).
- iMovie: simple basic option (free on Mac).
- **CapCut: easy for short-form and mobile workflows (free tier).**
- Filmora: user-friendly and affordable.

## Music principles
- Music sets tone, emotion, and pacing.
- For original content, lean toward timeless music, especially without lyrics.
- Choose one main emotional lane for the video.
- Prefer one song; do not exceed three unless very strong reason.
- Too many switches create emotional whiplash and can make the video feel like it ended in the middle.
