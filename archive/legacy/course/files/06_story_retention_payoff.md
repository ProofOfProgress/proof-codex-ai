# 06 — Story, Retention, Payoff, and Rewatch

Use when the creator needs scripting help, stronger pacing, better retention, a sharper ending, or intentional rewatchability.

## Semantic anchors
script, structure, retention, pacing, payoff, ending, story, rewatch, momentum, breadcrumbs

## Core story formats
- Setup, conflict, resolution
- Problem to solution
- Question then answer
- Before and after
- Surprise then reveal

## Cause and effect rule
Each beat naturally triggers the next. Use "but" and "so" as shorthand for movement.

## Retention checklist
- Everything must move closer to the promised end.
- Show progression clearly.
- Use countdowns, steps, or visible progress markers.
- Introduce twists or surprises along the way.
- Drop breadcrumbs reminding the viewer something is coming.

## Payoff rules
- Build anticipation for the payoff throughout.
- Deliver satisfying reveal, resolution, or emotional hit.
- Use twists only when they amplify memorability.
- End promptly after resolution.
- Save the best joke, reveal, or emotion for the end.

## Rewatchability tools
- Simple layers or Easter eggs
- Unexpected twists
- Emotional resonance
- Details that reward a replay
- Participation prompts

## Script checklist snapshot
- Is the hook shocking, unexpected, emotional, or intriguing?
- Does it establish expectations?
- Is there a reason to keep watching?
- Are visuals engaging and clear even without sound?
- Does every line build toward the payoff?
- Is the payoff clear, concise, and satisfying?
