# 09 — Growth, CTA, Selling, and Analytics

Use when the creator wants more subscribers, better CTA placement, sponsor integration, or analytics diagnosis.

## Semantic anchors
subscribers, CTA, selling, conversions, sponsor, brand integration, analytics, swipe-away, retention graph

## Turning viewers into subscribers
- Speak to one specific viewer, not a crowd.
- Use singular language when appropriate.
- Build a sense of community.
- Explain why subscribing helps the viewer.
- Place subscribe CTA right before the payoff, not after it.

## Selling without feeling salesy
- Use problem-solution approach.
- Integrate product naturally into the actual content idea.
- Be transparent and valuable.
- Avoid random trend hijacking unrelated to the product.
- Do not use weak if-then CTA formula when direct advice is clearer.
- Place product CTA right before payoff when attention is highest.

## Analytics priorities
- Focus mainly on view vs swipe-away and retention.
- ~70% viewed vs swiped away is a general benchmark; aim higher for broad entertainment.
- Push retention as high as possible; rewatches help.
- Use retention graph to spot drop-offs and flatline strengths.

## How to diagnose
- Low view vs swipe-away → likely hook or core idea problem.
- Viewers leave at specific moments → hypothesize what stalled momentum.
- Moments hold strong → repeat those strengths in future videos.
